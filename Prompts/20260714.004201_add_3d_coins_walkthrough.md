# Walkthrough: Adding 3D Shading to Coins

This document details the graphical improvements made to the coins in the Reversi board to simulate a 3D appearance.

## Actions Taken
1. **Identified Render Target:** Located the `onDraw` method inside `app/src/main/java/com/vypeensoft/reversi/view/BoardView.java` where the flat 2D coins were previously being drawn using `canvas.drawCircle()`.
2. **Applied Gradients for Shading:** 
   - Instead of a solid color, I applied a `android.graphics.RadialGradient` to both the Black and White `Paint` objects right before they are drawn on the canvas.
   - The center of the gradient is slightly offset to the top-left (`cx - radius/3`, `cy - radius/3`), which acts as a specular highlight, giving the illusion of a light source hitting a spherical object.
   - **Black coins** now transition from a soft gray (`#666666`) at the highlight to solid `Color.BLACK` at the edges.
   - **White coins** transition from solid `Color.WHITE` at the highlight to a darker gray (`#999999`) at the edges.
3. **Result:** The game board now features coins that visually pop off the screen with a smooth 3D shaded aesthetic.
