# Walkthrough: Flat Coin Rendering

This document explains the graphical adjustments made to convert the Reversi pieces from spheres into flat, plastic-style discs (like classic Othello pieces).

## Actions Taken
1. **Removed Gradients:** Located the `drawCoin` method in `app/src/main/java/com/vypeensoft/reversi/view/BoardView.java` and removed the `RadialGradient` logic, which was giving a marble/spherical illusion.
2. **Added Depth and Beveling:** Instead of a gradient, each piece is now drawn using three overlapping 2D shapes:
   - **Drop Shadow:** A translucent black circle offset slightly down and to the right (`cx + 3, cy + 4`) to give the piece physical presence on the board.
   - **Rim/Edge:** A full-size flat circle in a slightly lighter shade (e.g. `#333333` for black pieces).
   - **Inner Face:** A flat circle drawn at 85% of the radius using the pure solid color (`Color.BLACK` or `Color.WHITE`).
3. **Result:** This creates a clean bevel/rim effect, making the pieces look like identical flat discs (coins) exactly as one would expect from a physical board game or an app icon, completely maintaining the newly added 3D flip animation!
